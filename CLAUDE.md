## Voice & Tone Rules (CRITICAL)

### Don't make the site about Joy
- **Never use direct quotes from Joy** unless Joy explicitly approves them
- **Don't mention "Joy says" / "Joy describes" / "Joy puts it..."** — use plural/we-language
- **Avoid mentioning Joy's name repeatedly** across pages — she wants the focus on the team, not the founder
- **Exception:** Joy's own bio page (`/therapists/joy-bouchard/`) can be personal and reference her directly

### Future-proof for new hires
- **Avoid listing specific therapists by name** in service or modality pages
- Use plural language: *"Several of our clinicians..."* / *"Our therapists..."* / *"Some of our team..."*
- Goal: When a new therapist joins (next: August), content doesn't need rewriting
- **Exception:** Pages directly about a therapist's specialty (e.g., calling out Pam for trilingual care, Brynnlee for telehealth) — but only when explicitly relevant

### Use "we" language
- Replace "Joy says..." → "We hear..." / "We believe..."
- Replace "Joy describes her approach" → "We like to keep it human"
- Replace "Joy founded this..." → "Lotus Rose was founded..."

### Specific word preferences
- **"dialogue"** — not "dialog"
- **"women's issues"** — not "women's mental health in general"
- **Capitalize modality names** — Experiential, not experiential
- **"clinicians"** — used interchangeably with "therapists"; both fine

### Premium tone
- Warm, feminine, safe, inviting
- Room for humor — "the work is hard, the space should feel human"
- Don't apologize for the practice or pricing
- Emphasize flexibility: "There are no contracts"

## Accuracy Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Joy explicitly told us: **"Accuracy is VERY important here."**

### Don't fabricate
- Therapist details, conditions treated, training, credentials
- Patient stories or quotes
- Specific clinical claims
- When in doubt, ask Joy first

### Be careful with "trained" claims
- **Don't claim "trained in X"** unless the therapist has completed formal certification
- For modalities with extensive formal training programs (EMDR, ART, etc.) — "trained" is OK if confirmed
- For modalities someone has self-taught (e.g., IFS for some Lotus Rose therapists) — use *"Therapists who use IFS"* or *"Our therapists integrate IFS"* instead
- For modalities where ALL therapists have formal training (currently CBT, DBT, ACT, EFT, Experiential) — fine to say "all therapists are trained"

## Important Patterns

### Specialty chips link to modality/condition pages
- TherapistCard component has a `modalityMap` that auto-links specialties
- Don't bypass this — it's how we keep cross-linking automatic

### Header dropdown organization
- "Conditions We Treat" section + "Therapy Approaches" section
- Acronyms always show full names in parentheses: `EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing)`

### FAQ schema
- Every page with FAQs uses the `faqSchema` prop on layouts
- Schema generates automatically — don't hand-write it

### Schema graph
- All Person/Service/Therapy schema cross-references via `@id` to the LocalBusiness `@id`
- Don't break this graph by removing IDs
